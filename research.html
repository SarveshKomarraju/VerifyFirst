01 - Abstract

Understanding the mechanics behind Business Email Compromise.

Business Email Compromise (BEC) is one of the most financially damaging forms of cybercrime, relying on deception, impersonation, and social engineering rather than traditional technical exploitation.

This research examines how attackers manipulate organizational trust, exploit communication workflows, and use carefully crafted email-based attacks to influence victims into unauthorized actions.

Through analysis of attack patterns, security practices, and defensive strategies, VerifyFirst explores methods organizations can use to recognize and reduce the impact of BEC threats.

03 - Attack Lifecycle

How Business Email Compromise unfolds.

BEC attacks typically follow a predictable sequence of reconnaissance, deception, and manipulation designed to exploit trust.

01

Reconnaissance

Attackers gather information about employees, executives, vendors, and communication patterns.

02

Impersonation

Criminals mimic trusted identities using spoofed or compromised accounts.

03

Social Engineering

Victims are pressured through urgency, authority, and familiarity.

04

Fraud Request

Attackers request payments, credentials, or sensitive information.

05

Impact

Organizations experience financial, operational, and reputational damage.

04 - Research Findings

Key insights from the study.

01

Human trust remains the primary attack surface.

Unlike traditional cyberattacks that rely primarily on technical vulnerabilities, BEC attacks exploit communication habits, authority, and human decision-making.

02

Email authentication alone cannot prevent BEC.

Security controls such as email authentication standards reduce spoofing risks but cannot fully address compromised accounts or social engineering techniques.

03

Verification processes provide critical defense.

Organizations can significantly reduce risk by implementing verification procedures, employee awareness programs, and layered security controls.

05 - Defensive Strategy

Reducing BEC risk through layered defense.

Effective protection requires combining technology, procedures, and human awareness.

01

Verify

Confirm financial requests, credential changes, and unusual communications through separate trusted channels.

02

Educate

Build security awareness programs that teach employees to recognize manipulation and suspicious requests.

03

Protect

Implement email authentication, monitoring systems, and access controls to reduce attack impact.
