Understanding the Threat

What is
Business Email
Compromise?

Business Email Compromise (BEC) is a form of cybercrime that targets trust instead of technology. Attackers impersonate executives, vendors, or trusted contacts to manipulate victims into transferring money or revealing sensitive information.

BEC Definition

A targeted cyberattack in which an attacker uses fraudulent or compromised email accounts to deceive victims into taking unauthorized actions.

Understanding the Difference

Business Email Compromise is more than phishing.

While both attacks use email, Business Email Compromise focuses on manipulating trust rather than stealing credentials through malicious links.

Traditional Phishing

Broad, automated attacks

  • Mass email campaigns
  • Malicious links or attachments
  • Steals usernames and passwords
  • Little personalization
Business Email Compromise

Highly targeted deception

  • Researches specific organizations
  • Impersonates trusted people
  • Requests payments or sensitive data
  • Relies on psychology instead of malware
Attack Timeline

How a Business Email Compromise attack unfolds.

Although every incident is different, most Business Email Compromise attacks follow a predictable sequence of events designed to build trust before requesting money or sensitive information.

01
Reconnaissance

Research the target

Attackers gather information about employees, executives, vendors, payment procedures, and organizational relationships through public sources and previous breaches.

02
Preparation

Build credibility

Fake domains, spoofed email addresses, or compromised business accounts are prepared to closely resemble legitimate communications.

03
Social Engineering

Manipulate the victim

Urgency, authority, confidentiality, and trust are used to convince the victim to bypass normal verification procedures.

04
Financial Impact

Funds are transferred

Once the fraudulent request is approved, money or sensitive information is sent directly to the attacker.

Warning Signs

Six common indicators of Business Email Compromise.

Most BEC attacks contain subtle warning signs. Recognizing them before taking action can prevent financial loss and protect sensitive information.

!

Urgent Requests

Pressure to act immediately before verifying the request.

@

Suspicious Email Address

Minor spelling differences or unfamiliar domains pretending to be legitimate.

$

Payment Changes

Unexpected requests to change banking information or payment destinations.

👤

Executive Impersonation

Messages claiming to be from executives requesting confidential action.

🔒

Secrecy

Requests to keep transactions confidential or avoid normal approval procedures.

✓

Verification Discouraged

The sender discourages phone calls or independent confirmation.

Protection Framework

Five habits that dramatically reduce BEC risk.

Technology alone cannot stop Business Email Compromise. Effective defense combines secure systems, clear procedures, and informed people.

01

Always Verify Requests

Confirm payment requests, banking changes, and sensitive actions through a separate, trusted communication channel.

02

Enable Email Authentication

Implement SPF, DKIM, and DMARC to reduce domain spoofing and improve email trust.

03

Require Multi-Person Approval

High-value transactions should require approval from more than one authorized employee.

04

Train Employees Regularly

Awareness training helps employees recognize manipulation techniques before responding.

05

Report Suspicious Emails

Encourage reporting rather than punishment so potential attacks are investigated quickly.
